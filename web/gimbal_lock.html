This is a demonstration of gimbal lock using your implementation of rotations.py. The ZYX Euler angles are represented here as a 3-axis gimbal. The XYZ axes with white dots represent the transformed frame and rotate with the inner ring of the gimbal, while the axes on the far left represent the world frame. From the current orientation, you can perform a rotation operation about the yellow axis to observe the behavior of Euler angle interpolation at singularity.

Notice how the axes of the Z and X Euler angles (α and γ) are lined up. Since they rotate together, there are infinite combinations of α and γ that produce this current configuration. Try setting α and γ to 1, for example.

The gimbal is also unable to instaneously rotate about the world frame's X axis. Try performing a rotation about the X axis (see usage below); the gimbal has to rotate off axis in order to perform the rotation.

Key:

  • matrix - input a desired rotation matrix
  • rotation_angle - perform a rotation in radians about the yellow rotation axis
  • rotation_axis - direction of the yellow rotation axis
  • xyz_fixed_angles - input desired XYZ fixed angles
  • zyx_euler_angles - input desired ZYX Euler angles
